THE CONSTITUTION OF LEADERSHIP AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP ADVANCED DEVELOPMENT FOR POOR AND AVERAGE CHILDREN (LEADPAC FOUNDATION)

ARTICLE 1:

NAME AND IDENTITY The name of this organization shall be known and called Leadership & Entrepreneurship Advanced Development for Poor & Average Children (LEADPAC) Foundation a.k.a or herein after referred to as “Project20-Nigeria” for the purpose of vision design, direction and execution.


ARTICLE 2:

DEFINITION OF TERMS. In this constitution, the following words shall mean: -

  • (a) Vision: refers to the capacity to see farther than your eyes can look. Having a clear mental picture of the preferred future.
  • (b) Mission: refers to: strategic, tactical and operational plans for the implementation and execution of the Foundation’s policies, projects and programmes to meet the goals and objectives of the organization.
  • (c) Mindset: refers to a particular way of thinking associated with individual or a group of persons. It is an established set of attitudes held by a person or people.
  • (d) Leadership: refers to the capacity to influence others through inspiration, motivated by a passion, generated by a vision, produced by a conviction, ignited by a purpose. It is the power of influence through “character” impact.

    • ARTICLE 3:
  • LEADPAC VISION, MISSION, VALUES, IDEOLOGY AND GOAL
  • 3.1: Vision: to Nurture and Fulfill the Dream of a Transformed and Prosperous Nigeria.
  • 3.2: Mission: to raise, grow, develop, train, transform, educate and empower one million (1million) poor, average, vulnerable and less privileged teens and youths in each state of the Federation with pursuit for competence, diligence, dignity, sense of confident, peace loving, trustworthiness, honesty and good self-esteem across the country who are visionary, with good moral and spiritual standard, patriotic, humble and ever ready to fight for the unity, harmony, peaceful co-existence and the common good of Nigeria and Nigerians.
  • 3.3: Values: to maintain a good moral and spiritual lifestyle in all spheres of domestic and national life, conduct our businesses with high level of integrity and uprightness and serve our generation and nation in love, unity and understanding.
  • 3.4: Ideology: to discover the “WHY” (meaning & reasons) of our daily living, grow our potentials and invest our net worth into our society.
  • 3.5: LEADPAC CONTRIBUTION OF THE ORGANIZATION TO THE SOCIETY
  • 1. Increase in society stability and sustainable development.
  • 2. Reduction in examination malpractice and school drop-outs.
  • 3. Reduction in crimes, gangsters and cultism in our schools and society
  • 4. Creative and innovative mindset for job creation and employment.
  • 5. Conflict resolution and management for peaceful co-existence.ntrepreneurship: refers to an environment where these group of most poor and vulnerable persons (Teens and youths) in our society can acquire competence and skills in various trade and business development strategies in order to fit into the current diversified economic system of the country. Such skills shall include but not limited to trade skills, vocational skills, intellectual and entrepreneurial skills.
  • (f) Teamwork: refers to our framework based on a collection and harmonization of different thoughts and purposes from individual shared visions.
  • (g) Anti-corruption: refers the systematic and procedural fight and struggle against moral, material and financial impropriety or mismanagement and/or misappropriation of both natural, material, human and financial resources to the detriment of the general society, organization and persons.
  • (h) Good Governance: refers to the process and act of governing society and directing its government or state to ensure good functioning of the political institutions and state organs to foster economic development, social cohesion, corruption reduction and full citizens guaranteed participation, influence, oversight, responsive and responsible leadership for a harmonious societal development.
  • (i) Civil Society: refers to Non-government and not-for profit organizations (trade unions, faith and community based organizations) actively interested and engaged in development.
  • (j) Rights: refers to individual rights, civil, political, religious, minority, social, economic and cultural rights.
  • (k) BOT: Board of Trustees drawn from within and outside LEADPAC Foundation.
  • (l) Ignite: refers to Leadership and Academic/Career Projects for students in post-Primary Schools across Nigeria.
  • (m) Teens Lead Africa(TLA): refers to Leadership and Entrepreneurship/Skills Projects for teenagers who have graduated from the Secondary Schools and not yet in the higher institutions.


    • ARTICLE 4

    OBJECTIVES OF LEADPAC FOUNDATION

  • 4.1: to grow, develop and train young generations into True and Effective Leaders for the society and Nigeria government at local, state and national levels.
  • 4.1.1: to encourage and promote academic and career excellence through effective mentorship, educational research and advocacy
  • 4.1.2: to develop, train and empower young minds in the areas of entrepreneurship and vocational skills for job creation and reduction of unemployment and poverty.
  • 4.1.3: to collaborate and partner with relevant stakeholders on Teens and Youths development for capacity building, sensitization, undertake advocacy and expansion.
  • 4.1.4: to collaborate with media organizations to effectively cover wide areas of our society with our activities across Nigeria.

    • ARTICLE 5

    MEMBERSHIP OF LEADPAC FOUNDATION

  • 5.1 Membership of the organization is open to all citizens of the Federal Republic of Nigeria; and on condition for other Africans. Age bracket at entry shall be between 10 and 25years only.
  • 5.1.1 Registration shall be by obtaining a certified organization Registration Form signed by the Country President on a fee with evidence of citizenship of Nigeria. (photocopy of personal and/or parent’s citizenship valid Identity Card attached to the form)
  • 5.1.2. The organization shall also accept temporary membership (Volunteers) at no fee as co-workers on an agreed terms and conditions whenever need arises.
  • 5.1.3. Forfeiture of Membership: A person shall cease or loss his/her membership status is his/her conduct is found to be inimical (as specified in the registration form) and to the goal and objectives of the organization referred to in Articles 3 & 4 as shall be determined by a joint committee of the BOT and Leadership/Management Team of the organization.

    • ARTICLE 6:

    COMPOSITION OF LEADPAC FOUNDATION BOARD OF TRUSTEES

  • 6.1. The Board of Trustees shall consist of 9 members who shall be drawn from within and outside the organization. This shall be individuals who have shown interest in promoting teenagers and youths development for economic and socio-political transformation, stability and welfare of Nigeria and Nigerians
  • 6.1.1 The Chairman, Vice Chairman shall be elected from amongst the Board Members at its inaugural session by simple majority for the period of five (5) years one term.
  • 6.1.2 The Board Secretary shall not be elected or selected amongst the members, but shall be the Country Secretary of LEADPAC Foundation with no tenure as long as he/she holds that office in the organization.
  • 6.1.3 The Country President/Founder, Executive Director/Admin and The Project Director – Project20 Dream shall be ordinary members at the Board sessions with no power of vote.
  • 6.1.4 Quorum of the Board shall not be less than six (6) members (two third) of the Board at any given meeting, not counting the ordinary members as specified in article
  • 6.1.5 The Board shall endeavour to reach all decisions by consensus, but if necessary a vote shall be taken on any vital issue(s), which vote will be carried by simple majority. The Chairman shall have a casting vote.
  • 6.1.6 The Board shall be entitled to regulate the manner in which the meetings of the Board shall be conducted.
  • 6.1.7 The Board of Trustees shall meet twice a year. The last meeting shall be held at least 30days to the Annual General Meeting.
  • 6.1.8 Where necessary, The Chairman shall call for an emergency meeting with not more than fourteen (14) days’ notice.

    • ARTICLE 7:

    RESPONSIBILITIES AND FUNCTIONS OF THE BOARD OF TRUSTEES

  • 7.1 The BOT shall be responsible for the registration of the organization with all relevant authorities and other local and international bodies that may be beneficial to the organization’s vision and mission.
  • 7.1.1 The BOT shall in a joint consultative and strategic meeting with the Leadership/Management Team of the organization be responsible for policy formulation of LEADPAC Foundation subject to ratification by the Annual General Meeting.
  • 7.1.2 The BOT and the Leadership/Management Team shall be responsible for the raising of fund for the organization through legitimate means only.
  • 7.1.3 The BOT shall hold the properties of LEADPAC Foundation in TRUST.
  • 7.1.4 The BOT Chairman shall through the Board Secretary cause a meeting of the Board to be convened.
  • 7.1.5 In a situation where the Chairman fails to convene a statutory meeting of the Board when due or necessary, majority of the BOT shall cause a meeting of the BOT to be convened through the Vice Chairman and/or the Board Secretary.
  • 7.1.6 Notice of an ordinary, emergency or special meeting of the Board shall be in writing, specifying the time, place and business to be conducted at the meeting; not less than fourteen (14) days before the date of the meeting; except for special meetings.
  • 7.1.7 The BOT Chairman shall preside over the meeting and in his/her absence, the Vice Chairman shall preside.
  • 7.1.8 Subject to the approval of the Board, the Chairman may invite any person to attend a meeting of the Board and take part in its deliberations, but such person may not be entitled to vote.
  • 7.1.9 The BOT shall serve as an advisory body to the organization in matters affecting the organization, dispute resolution, infractions; and in matter of affiliation, collaboration and partnership with both local and international bodies in fulfilling her vision and mission.

    • ARTICLE 8:

    LEADPAC FOUNDATION ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING

  • 8.1 The Annual General Meeting (AGM) of LEADPAC Foundation shall be the supreme decision making body of the organization.
  • 8.1.1 The AGM of LEADPAC Foundation shall hold 60days after the end of the organization financial year to consider the annual and audited reports.
  • 8.1.2 The AGM shall ratify the appointments of the Leadership/Management Team of the organization; including the Coordinating Teams of the arms and Heads and staff of the various Research and Training Centres of LEADPAC Foundation across the nation – Nigeria.
  • 8.1.3 Notice of the AGM shall be sent to the accredited delegates of LEADPAC Foundation by the Country Secretary not less than fourteen (14) days prior to the Annual General Meeting, specifying the time, place and agenda to be conducted at the meeting.
  • 8.1.4 The Annual General Meeting shall be presided by the Country President/Founder of LEADPAC Foundation. In his/her absence, the BOT Chairman shall and in his/her absence, the Executive Director/Admin shall preside over the AGM.

    • ARTICLE 9:

    EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR/ADMIN, COUNTRY SECRETARY AND OTHER EMPLOYEES

  • 9.a The Executive Director/Admin shall be the Chief Implementing Officer (CIO)of the organization responsible for the day-to-day running of LEADPAC Foundation projects, programmes, including oversight function of the Research & Training Centres and policies execution across the nation.
  • 9.b The Executive Director/Admin shall preside over the Administrative Meetings of the organization comprising all Policies Implementing Units (PIU)of LEADPAC Foundation.
  • 9.1a The Country Secretary shall be responsible for the day-to-day running of the organization’s national secretariat, coordinate and supervise the states and L.G.A. chapter offices.
  • 9.1b The Country Secretary shall perform any other responsibilities that may be assigned to him or her by the Executive Director/Admin, BOT Chairman, Country President/Founder and AGM.
  • 9.2a The Executive Director/Admin and Country Secretary shall hold office for five (5) years subject to renewal for another term of five(5) years.
  • 9.2b There shall be other offices that shall be on a part time employment and/or volunteering basis. Such employable offices shall include The Budget & Finance Officer and Other Secretariat personnel both at the National, States and Local Government Areas. Heads and staff of various Research and Training Centres across the country The Arms (Ignite, Teens’ Lead Africa and African Youths Network) Team Coordinators and other Offices. Volunteers and Stakeholders.

    • ARTICLE 10.

    PROJECT INERTIA, PROJECT BUILT-IN, PROJECT ENTREPRENEUR & PROJECT-COMMUNITY & PROJECT20-GOVERNANCE.

  • 10.1 Project20-Inertia: shall be the organization Leadership Growth, Development and Training Initiative of LEADPAC Foundation including but not limited to the Teenagers Leadership & Entrepreneurship Development Academy, Teens Business School, African Undergraduates Leadership Research Project and The Institute for the 4th Level Professional Leadership Development and Anti-Corruption Studies.
  • 10.2 Project20-Built-In: shall be the organization’s Library Services including Teenagers Book/Reader’s Society, Students’ Academic/Career Mentorship & Counseling Projects, Secondary School Educational Research Projects and a Teens Leadership & Entrepreneurship Research Library Complex with employable staff.
  • 10.3 Project20-Entrepreneur: shall be the organization’s Skills Acquisition, Empowerment & Training Complex with employable staff.
  • 10.4 Project20-Community: shall be the organization’s Corporate Social Responsibility platform to give back to the society especially in rural and semi-urban communities.
  • 10.5 Project20-Governance: shall be the Organization’s Socio-Political and Economic Initiatives for engaging the government of the day on Good Governance, Budget Tracking and Policies formulations.

    • ARTICLE 11:

    LEGAL STATUS OF LEADPAC FOUNDATION

  • 11.0 The LEADPAC Foundation shall be a distinct legal entity with the power to acquire, hold and alienate properties of any kind and with the power to acquire rights and incur obligations and have perpetual succession.

    • ARTICLE 12:

    NON PROFIT MAKING STATUS

  • 12.1 LEADPAC Foundation is not formed and does not exist for the purpose of carrying out any business that has as its object the making of gain or profit.
  • 12.2 The income and assets of LEADPAC Foundation shall be solely for the promotion and execution of the objectives for which it is established and operated
  • 12.3 No part of the income or assets of LEADPAC Foundation shall be paid, directly or indirectly, by way of dividend to any person(s) and/or organization(s).

    • ARTICLE 13:

    LEADPAC FOUNDATION FINANCE

  • 13.0 FINANCIAL PLAN The Leadership / Management Team and the Board of Trustees of our organization are by our constitution empowered to attract sponsorships and donors from both local and foreign bodies to our core activities; while they equally contribute financially to the success of our events and/or projects that we may have. Others include: 1. LEADPAC Foundation Profile Fee 2. Membership Registration Fee 3. Special Annual Project Contribution 4. Individual & Corporate Sponsorship 5. LEADPAC Foundation Business Team (Entrepreneurship Unit) 6. Leadership & Financial Advisory Board 7. Support from Foundations/ Foreign Bodies 8. Multi-Nationals 9. Government (Local, State and Federal)
  • 13.1 The income of LEADPAC Foundation shall be derived from sponsorships, registration, dues, special project contributions, domestic and foreign donations/aid, interest on investments and savings.
  • 13.2 The financial year of LEADPAC Foundation shall be from 1st January to 31st December.
  • 13.3 All monies received by or on behalf of LEADPAC Foundation shall be paid into LEADPAC Foundation accounts within 48 hours or 72 hours where there are intervening public holidays or weekends.
  • 13.4 Full and proper financial records and books shall be kept by the Leadership/Management through the office of Finance & Budget for all financial transaction of the network.
  • 13.5 (a) There shall be a six (6) member finance team comprising the Organization’s Executive Director, Accountant, Internal Auditor, The Vice Chairman of BOT, Treasurer and one member that shall be appointed by the Country President/Founder. (b) There shall be four (4) signatories to all LEADPAC Foundation accounts including Chairman BOT and The Country President in category A while Country Secretary and Accountant shall be in category B. Category A shall be the principal signatories. At least one of them must sign the cheque(s) with any person from category B. (c) Cheque(s) must be cleared from the office of the Country President/Founder and documented at the Office of the Executive Director/Admin before going to the bank for withdrawal(s) and/or lodgment(s)

    • ARTICLE 14:

    RESIGNATION FROM LEADPAC FOUNDATION

  • 14.1 All resignation from BOT shall be addressed to the Chairman of the BOT through the Board Secretary.
  • 14.2 In the case of the Chairman of BOT resignation, he/she shall address it to the Vice Chairman through the Board Secretary.
  • 14.3 In the case of the Executive Director/Admin and Country Secretary, he/she shall address the letter to the Country President/Founder. For other staff of the organization, the letter shall be written to the Executive Director/Admin through the Country Secretary after which an emergency Administrative Meeting shall be convened by the Executive Director/Admin through the Country Secretary.

    • ARTICLE 15:

    AMENDMENTS TO THE CONSTITUTION

  • 15.0 LEADPAC Foundation may by a resolution of two third of the Annual General Meeting Delegates amend any provision(s) of this constitution.

    • ARTICLE 16:

    DISSOLUTION OF LEADPAC FOUNDATION

  • 16.1 LEADPAC Foundation shall cease to operate if at least two third of the Annual General Meeting Delegates shall prove without any reasonable doubts that the vision, mission and objectives of the organization has been bridged. The BOT chairman shall then meet to dissolve the organization.
  • 16.2 In the event of a dissolution, the assets and monies in all the organization’s accounts remaining after the discharge of liabilities shall be disposed of the Board of Trustees in such manner as the Board may, by resolution determine, provided that no property(s) and monies whatsoever shall be given or transferred to or distributed to any individuals, but it shall be given or transferred to some other institution(s) and/or organization(s) involved in poor, average, disadvantaged, vulnerable and less privileged teenagers & youths leadership and entrepreneurship development/training in Nigeria and which similarly prohibits the distribution of its assets and monies in all accounts amongst its Leadership/Members or Trustees whatever the case may be.
